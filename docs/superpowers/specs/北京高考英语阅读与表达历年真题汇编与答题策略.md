## 第一部分 题型历史沿革

### 1. 首次出现年份

**北京高考英语"阅读与表达"题型于2021年首次出现。**

在2020年及以前，北京高考英语试卷的"书面表达"部分第一节为**情景作文**（分值15分），通常要求考生根据四幅图的先后顺序写一篇周记或看图写话。

**2021年，北京高考英语进行题型改革**：书面表达第一节由"情景作文"调整为**"阅读与表达"**（分值12分），要求考生阅读一篇短文后，根据题目要求用英文回答问题。这一改革标志着北京卷从单纯的"写作输出"向"读写结合"转变，更加强调在真实语境中获取信息、处理信息并进行书面表达的综合能力。

### 2. 题型位置与分值演变

| 年份 | 题型位置 | 题量 | 分值 | 备注 |
|------|----------|------|------|------|
| 2020及以前 | 书面表达第一节：情景作文 | 1题 | 15分 | 看图写话/周记 |

### 3. 全国视野下的"阅读表达"

"阅读表达"类题型并非北京首创。早在新世纪初，湖南、山东、上海、天津等省份就已采用这种读写结合的开放型答题方式。北京卷在2021年引入该题型，是对全国高考英语改革趋势的回应，体现了从"知识立意"向"能力立意"、从"单一技能考查"向"综合素养考查"的转变。

## 第二部分 总体答题策略指导

### 一、题型本质认知

"阅读与表达"不是单纯的阅读理解题，也不是自由的作文题，而是**"带着镣铐跳舞"的读写综合题**。它要求考生在准确理解语篇的基础上，用**自己的语言**（或原文信息）进行精准、简洁、完整的书面表达。

### 二、四大核心能力要求

1. **信息定位能力**：快速在原文中找到与问题对应的句子或段落
2. **信息转述能力**：将原文信息转化为符合语法规范、满足字数要求的答案
3. **批判性思维能力**：对给定陈述进行正误判断并解释原因（第42题）
4. **开放性表达能力**：基于文本进行合理延伸，发表个人观点（第43题）

### 三、分题型的答题策略

#### 第40题：信息查找题（2分）

**特征**：通常是What/Why/How开头的细节题，答案可直接在原文中找到。

**答题策略**：
- **指读定位法**：用手指或笔尖逐行扫描，找到与题干关键词完全对应或同义替换的句子
- **完整提取**：时间、地点、原因等修饰成分要一并提取，不可遗漏（如2021年30题"In January, 2011"不能只写"In 2011"）
- **允许摘录**：可以直接使用原文句子回答，无需改写
- **控制字数**：通常1-2句话即可，不要过度发挥

**常见失分点**：
- 信息提取不完整（漏时间、漏地点、漏原因）
- 照抄时把无关信息也抄进去
- 语法错误（时态、主谓一致、介词搭配）

#### 第41题：细节概括题（2分）

**特征**：通常要求概括某事的原因、方式、过程或结果，答案可能分散在原文多处。

**答题策略**：
- **锁定时间/逻辑锚点**：注意题干中的before/after/when/because等限定词
- **动作链梳理**：如果是过程题，按时间顺序梳理动作链（如"built → drew → sent"）
- **并列信息不遗漏**：如果原文提到两个并列原因或方式，答案中必须都出现
- **同义转换**：适当进行词汇替换（如原文"my need for independence"可转换为"The author's desire to be independent"）

**常见失分点**：
- 只答一点，遗漏并列信息
- 用自己的话概括时偏离原文意思
- 逻辑关系颠倒（因果倒置）

#### 第42题：批判性判断题（3分）

**特征**：给出一个陈述句，要求考生判断哪一部分是错误的，划线并解释原因。

**答题策略**：
- **三步拆弹法**：
  1. 将陈述句拆分为若干信息单元（主语、谓语、宾语、原因状语等）
  2. 逐单元回原文核对
  3. 找出与原文矛盾的那个单元
- **划线规范**：用横线明确标出错误部分（如 *"because publishable data had been produced"*）
- **解释精准**：解释时直接引用原文依据，说明"原文说……，而题干说……，两者矛盾"
- **不扩大打击面**：只指出错误部分，不要否定整个句子

**常见失分点**：
- 划线部分不准确（划多了或划少了）
- 解释时只说"与原文不符"，不给出具体原文依据
- 解释中出现新的语法错误

#### 第43题：开放表达题（5分）

**特征**：要求考生结合文本内容，就某一主题发表个人观点，通常有字数限制（In about 40 words）。

**答题策略**：
- **点名+给理由**：先明确回答题目问题（Who/What/Do you agree?），再给出2-3个理由
- **紧扣原文**：理由必须能从原文中找到支撑，不能天马行空
- **字数控制**：35-45词为最佳区间。少于30词内容单薄，多于50词容易出错
- **语法保稳**：用简单句+1个从句即可，不要追求复杂长句
- **使用连接词**：Firstly, Besides, Therefore, In this way 等使逻辑清晰

**万能框架（40词版）**：
> **赞同/学习类**：___ impressed me most. Despite having no ___ (背景劣势), he/she ___ (具体行动) through ___ (品质). This proves ___ (升华).
>
> **观点类**：I agree/disagree. Firstly, ___ (理由1). Besides, ___ (理由2). Therefore, ___ (结论).

**常见失分点**：
- 不回答题目问题，直接开始论述
- 理由与原文脱节
- 字数严重超标或不足
- 语法错误密集

### 四、考场时间分配与答题规范

1. **时间分配**：阅读与表达整体建议留足**10-12分钟**。其中阅读短文2分钟，答题8-10分钟。
2. **答题区域**：必须在答题卡指定区域作答，超出区域无效。
3. **书写规范**：字迹工整，大小写正确，标点符号规范。
4. **绝不空白**：31、32题（或40-43题）是送分题，即使时间紧张，也要写满简单句，**空白=自动放弃**。

### 五、日常训练建议

1. **精读近5年真题**：2021-2025年的5篇真题覆盖了说明文、记叙文两种主要体裁，务必逐题精做，对照满分答案分析差距。
2. **建立"阅读表达错题本"**：按"信息遗漏/语法错误/逻辑偏差/字数失控"四类归类错因，每周复盘。
3. **限时训练**：每天1篇，严格限时10分钟（阅读2分钟+答题8分钟），培养考场节奏感。
4. **面批面改**：开放题（43题）务必找老师面批，明确"内容分"和"语言分"的得失。
5. **背诵积累**：背诵5-8篇满分答案，积累"Firstly, Besides, Therefore, In this way"等连接词和"It proves that... This means..."等总结句式。

## 第三部分 历年真题汇编（2021-2025）

### 2021年北京高考英语阅读与表达

**【短文】**（约322词，说明文，人与社会）

Recent research suggests that if an argument gets resolved, the emotional response tied to it is significantly reduced or almost completely erased. Thus, it may be worth bringing up issues with your friends, family members, or classmates rather than holding them back.

There is a difference between arguing and fighting. Arguing is that you and your opponent present your concerns and discuss the feelings and issues related to those concerns. You can engage in an argument respectfully without stirring up (激起) anger. Fighting, however, usually involves personal attacks, raising of voices, and storming out.

Discussing your issues and resolving them instead of stuffing them down can improve your emotional health. In a study, 2,000 people were asked to record their feelings and experiences for eight days in a row. When people had an argument that they considered resolved, they had half the reactivity (情绪反应) of those who avoided an argument. Reactivity is an increase in negative emotions or a decrease in positive emotions. In other words, resolving an argument cuts your negative feelings by half. One day later, people who had a resolved argument reported no increase of negative emotions compared with those who avoided an argument. This means that resolving an argument can feel like you have reached a state of resolution—and you are less likely to be annoyed.

Moreover, the older you are, the more likely you will come to a resolution after an argument. This may be because more life experience usually leads to more defined priorities. You are more likely to distinguish between what matters and what does not.

It is easier to avoid a discussion, but risking talking about it may eventually lead to a better outcome.

**【题目】**

40. According to this passage, what is arguing? (2分)
41. Why is it that "the older you are, the more likely you will come to a resolution after an argument"? (2分)
42. Please decide which part is false in the following statement, then underline it and explain why. (3分)
   Resolving arguments can improve your emotional health, because it increases your reactivity and reduces the chance of you getting angry.
43. In addition to improving emotional health, what do you think are some other benefits of resolving an argument? (In about 40 words) (5分)

### 2022年北京高考英语阅读与表达

**【短文】**（约286词，记叙文，人与社会）

Tom, a 15-year-old inventor and entrepreneur (创业者), witnessed at his own school the widespread consumption of sugary drinks by kids. He knew there had to be a better portable drink solution and decided to innovate from something he saw in his own home: fruit infused (浸泡) water.

Tom watched his mum make healthy fruit infusions but then struggle for a take-along option. From observing his mum and from his desire to give kids better drink options, he came up with his original model for the Fun Bottle. "I wanted to come up with a healthy, natural way for people to drink when on the go. A big part of my mission is to get people of all ages off sugary drinks," Tom explains.

The bottle is made with a strainer (滤网) that allows the great tastes and natural sugars of the various fruits and vegetables you choose to come through the water, without any of the seeds or skins flowing through.

Tom is proud of his design and excited to be selling the Fun Bottle on his website and in stores, but this 15-year-old is most proud of the opportunities that Fun Bottle presents to others. It helps to provide healthy alternatives to sugary drinks; and also Tom donates part of the profits to the Organisation for a Healthier Generation (OHG).

Tom has been awarded several prizes, but this teenage innovator remains humble. When asked what advice he'd give other entrepreneurial youth, he says, "Prepare and have your family's support. It is important to know from the beginning that there are a lot of highs and lows, and there is no such thing as overnight success."

**【题目】**

40. What did Tom witness at his own school? (2分)
41. Where did Tom get the idea for the original model for the Fun Bottle? (2分)
42. Please decide which part is false in the following statement, then underline it and explain why. (3分)
   Tom is most proud of the opportunities that Fun Bottle presents to others because he not only provides healthy alternatives to sugary drinks but also donates all the profits to the OHG.
43. Among Tom's qualities, which one(s) do you think will be important for us? Why? (In about 40 words) (5分)

### 2023年北京高考英语阅读与表达

**【短文】**（约280词，说明文，人与社会）

Habit formation is the process by which behaviours become automatic. People develop countless habits as they explore the world, whether they are aware of them or not. Understanding how habits take shape may be helpful in changing bad habits.

Habits are built through learning and repetition. A person is thought to develop a habit in the course of pursuing goals by beginning to associate certain cues (刺激) with behavioural responses that help meet the goal. Over time, thoughts of the behaviour and ultimately the behaviour itself are likely to be triggered (触发) by these cues.

A "habit loop (环)" is a way of describing several related elements that produce habits. These elements are called the cue, the routine, and the reward. For example, stress could serve as a cue that one responds to by eating, which produces the reward—the reduction of stress. While a routine involves repeated behaviour, it's not necessarily performed in response to a deep-rooted urge, as a habit is.

Old habits can be difficult to shake, and healthy habits are often harder to develop. But through repetition, it's possible to form new habits. The amount of time needed to build a habit will depend on multiple factors, including the individual and the intended behaviour. While you are able to pick up a new habit in a few weeks, it takes many months to build a healthy habit.

Take some time to think about what leads to bad habits and re-evaluate what you get out of them (or don't). Consider and keep in mind why you want to make a change, including how the change reflects your values.

**【题目】**

40. How are habits built? (2分)
41. In what way is a routine different from a habit? (2分)
42. Please decide which part is false in the following statement, then underline it and explain why. (3分)
   Picking up a new habit takes a few weeks, while building a healthy habit takes a shorter time.
43. What benefit(s) have you got from one of your good habits? (In about 40 words) (5分)

### 2024年北京高考英语阅读与表达

**【短文】**（约268词，记叙文，人与自我）

Growing up, I idealised independence. I always wanted my own efforts to be enough. When I decided to pursue a postgraduate degree, I wanted to develop a novel research programme and quickly establish myself as an independent scientist. But I was unrealistically optimistic about what I could achieve.

As I began designing experiments, my committee members warned me about the challenges I would face. But my need for independence drove me to push forward with my research plan. As a result, the first four years of my postgraduate career were defined by a series of failures.

During my second year, I failed my comprehensive exam because my proposal was unclear. During my third year, I discovered that after treating thousands of seeds, I obtained just one plant I could use for experiments. By my fourth year, my desperation to succeed overshadowed my desire for independence.

My adviser and I devised (想出) a somewhat unusual solution: I would spend three months in a collaborating (合作的) lab to obtain specialised training. I worked extensively with other students, constantly asked questions, and helped with ongoing projects to learn everything I could. Finally, I conducted an elegant experiment that would not have been possible without the help of the members in the lab.

My adviser saw this experience as a groundbreaking success, emphasising the collaborating skills I acquired. A few months later, when I repeated the experiment in my home lab, I produced more publishable data. By learning when to ask for help, I eventually found myself on the way to becoming an independent scientist.

**【题目】**

40. In the beginning, what drove the author to push forward with the research plan? (2分)
41. What was the solution by the adviser and the author after those repeated failures? (3分)
42. Please decide which part is false in the following statement, then underline it and explain why. (3分)
   The adviser considered the author's experience in the lab a groundbreaking success because publishable data had been produced.
43. From this story, what can you learn about "independence"? (In about 40 words) (5分)

### 2025年北京高考英语阅读与表达

**【短文】**（约280词，记叙文，人与社会）

There's something magical about the way imagination works. Around the world, kids are using imagination in ways that were once only found in science fiction.

Take the story of Mark Lee. He loved biking around his neighbourhood but grew upset seeing that many plastic bottles littered the parks and sidewalks. Instead of simply complaining, he decided to do something about it.

Using an old 3D printer, Mark started experimenting. He melted (熔化) plastic bottles down and tried shaping them into small bricks. At first, everything went wrong—the plastic broke, the printer jammed, and the pieces didn't fit together. Friends laughed a little, and even Mark's parents thought he should just give up.

But Mark had a different idea. He redesigned the bricks to lock together like puzzle (拼图) pieces and adjusted the heating temperature carefully. After months of trial and error, he succeeded. With a little help from his school and the local community centre, Mark used his bricks to build a small bike shelter for his neighbourhood.

And he's not alone. Thirteen-year-old Sally Carter loved reading fantasy books. Inspired by magical maps in her stories, she wondered: what if we could build a real-world app that helps people explore hidden places in their own cities? She gathered friends, learned some basic programming, and together they created an app that maps small local parks and gardens few people knew about. Their app quickly grew popular in town and even won a local youth innovation award.

Next time you see a young person lost in thought, don't rush to pull them back. They might just be imagining the next great idea.

**【题目】**

40. What upset Mark when he was biking around his neighbourhood? (2分)
41. How does the app created by Sally and her friends help? (2分)
42. Please decide which part is false in the following statement, then underline it and explain why. (3分)
   With encouragement from his friends and parents, Mark built a small bike shelter for his neighbourhood after months of trial and error.
43. How can you put your imagination into practice as Mark and Sally did? (In about 40 words) (5分)

## 第四部分 参考答案与解析

### 2021年参考答案与解析

**40. According to this passage, what is arguing?**

**参考答案**：Arguing is discussing your worries, related feelings and problems with the other party. / Arguing is that you and your opponent present your concerns and discuss the feelings and issues related to those concerns.

**解析**：答案线索在第二段第二句"Arguing is that you and your opponent present your concerns and discuss the feelings and issues related to those concerns." 直接摘录或同义转换均可。注意使用动名词或从句结构，保持语法正确。

**41. Why is it that "the older you are, the more likely you will come to a resolution after an argument"?**

**参考答案**：It is because more life experience usually leads to more defined priorities. / Because more life experience may help people better identify priorities in life.

**解析**：答案线索在第四段第二句"This may be because more life experience usually leads to more defined priorities." 注意将"defined priorities"转换为"identify priorities"或直接引用原文。

**42. Please decide which part is false in the following statement, then underline it and explain why.**

**陈述**：Resolving arguments can improve your emotional health, because it increases your reactivity and reduces the chance of you getting angry.

**参考答案**：
- **错误部分**：*because it increases your reactivity*
- **解释**：According to the passage, resolving arguments helps to **decrease** your reactivity, not increase it. When people had a resolved argument, they had half the reactivity of those who avoided an argument. / Resolving an argument cuts your negative feelings by half, which means it reduces reactivity rather than increasing it.

**解析**：第三段明确指出"resolving an argument cuts your negative feelings by half"，且"had half the reactivity"，因此"increases your reactivity"与原文矛盾。划线要精准，解释要引用原文依据。

**43. In addition to improving emotional health, what do you think are some other benefits of resolving an argument?**

**参考答案**：
- **One possible version**: Resolving an argument allows us to face and handle disagreements, during which we discuss our differences and work through them together. This leads to positive changes and facilitates our goal achievement. Besides, it's a precious chance to develop communicative skills and problem-solving skills.
- **Another version**: It helps us understand each other better and build stronger relationships. Also, it improves our communication skills and teaches us how to solve problems peacefully.

**解析**：先回答问题（other benefits），给出两点理由（促进目标实现+培养沟通能力/增进理解），使用连接词使逻辑清晰，字数约40词。理由需言之成理，不必拘泥于一种答案。

**42. Please decide which part is false in the following statement, then underline it and explain why.**

**参考答案**：
- **错误部分**：*donates all the profits to the OHG*
- **解释**：According to the passage, Tom donates **part of the profits** to the OHG, not all the profits. / The passage says "Tom donates part of the profits to the Organisation for a Healthier Generation (OHG)", so "all the profits" is false.

**解析**：第四段最后一句明确说"Tom donates part of the profits"，题干中"all"与原文"part"矛盾。划线要精准到"all"，解释要直接引用原文。

**参考答案**：
- **One possible version**: **Creativity**. Tom designed the Fun Bottle which helps provide healthy alternatives to sugary drinks, witnessing the widespread consumption of sugary drinks by kids. It's creativity that solves many problems in our daily life, and creation is what we need most.
- **Another version**: **Perseverance and social responsibility**. Tom kept trying to find a healthy drink solution and donated part of profits to OHG. These qualities help us overcome difficulties and contribute to society.

**解析**：先点名品质（Creativity/Perseverance/Social responsibility等），再结合原文细节解释（witnessed... designed...），最后升华（solves problems / contributes to society）。字数控制在40词左右。

### 2023年参考答案与解析

**40. How are habits built?**

**参考答案**：Habits are built through learning and repetition. / Through learning and repetition.

**解析**：直接摘录第二段第一句，最稳妥。注意介词"through"不可遗漏。

**41. In what way is a routine different from a habit?**

**参考答案**：A routine is not necessarily performed in response to a deep-rooted urge, as a habit is. / Unlike a habit, a routine doesn't necessarily respond to a deep-rooted urge.

**解析**：答案线索在第三段最后一句。注意"not necessarily"和"deep-rooted urge"是关键词，不可遗漏或替换为不准确的说法。

**42. Please decide which part is false in the following statement, then underline it and explain why.**

**陈述**：Picking up a new habit takes a few weeks, while building a healthy habit takes a shorter time.

**参考答案**：
- **错误部分**：*building a healthy habit takes a shorter time*
- **解释**：According to the passage, while you are able to pick up a new habit in a few weeks, it takes **many months** to build a healthy habit. So building a healthy habit takes a **longer** time, not a shorter time.

**解析**：第四段最后一句明确对比：new habit = a few weeks; healthy habit = many months。题干说"shorter"与原文"many months"矛盾，属于比较关系颠倒。

**43. What benefit(s) have you got from one of your good habits?**

**参考答案**：
- **One possible version**: I run for half an hour after school every day, which benefits me a lot. Firstly, running can help me get fit and prevent diseases. What's more, it will provide relief from aches or tension that I may be suffering due to stress.
- **Another version**: Reading books before bed helps me relax and expand my knowledge. It improves my sleep quality and broadens my horizons, making me more thoughtful.

**解析**：先点名好习惯（running/reading等），再分两点说明好处（健康+减压/知识+睡眠），使用连接词Firstly, What's more。注意用第一人称，结合个人真实体验更佳，但语法正确优先。

### 2024年参考答案与解析

**40. In the beginning, what drove the author to push forward with the research plan?**

**参考答案**：The author's need for independence. / His/Her desire for independence. / The need to be independent.

**解析**：答案线索在第二段第二句"But my need for independence drove me to push forward with my research plan." 直接摘录主语部分即可，注意人称转换（原文my→答案the author's/his/her）。

**41. What was the solution by the adviser and the author after those repeated failures?**

**参考答案**：The author would spend three months in a collaborating lab to obtain specialised training. / The solution was that the author went to a collaborating lab for three months to get specialised training.

**解析**：答案线索在第四段第一句。注意完整提取"three months"、"collaborating lab"和"specialised training"三个关键信息，缺一不可。

**42. Please decide which part is false in the following statement, then underline it and explain why.**

**陈述**：The adviser considered the author's experience in the lab a groundbreaking success because publishable data had been produced.

**参考答案**：
- **错误部分**：*because publishable data had been produced*
- **解释**：According to the passage, the adviser considered it a groundbreaking success because the author **acquired collaborating skills**, not because publishable data had been produced. The publishable data came a few months later when the author repeated the experiment in the home lab.

**解析**：第五段第一句明确说"emphasising the collaborating skills I acquired"，而"publishable data"是之后在家实验室重复实验时才产生的，时间线和因果关系均错误。划线要精准到原因状语从句。

**43. From this story, what can you learn about "independence"?**

**参考答案**：
- **One possible version**: Firstly, being independent doesn't mean simply doing anything by oneself without cooperating with others. Instead, skills acquired from a collaborative environment are essential for developing independence. Additionally, independence is a tendency to tackle difficult problems by our own strength, rather than rely on others forever.
- **Another version**: True independence means knowing when to ask for help. Collaboration skills are essential for real independence, and we should learn from others while maintaining self-direction.

**解析**：先否定对"independence"的误解（≠不合作/≠单打独斗），再给出正面定义（合作中培养+自主解决问题+知道何时求助），体现辩证思维。字数控制在40词左右，使用连接词使逻辑清晰。

### 2025年参考答案与解析

**40. What upset Mark when he was biking around his neighbourhood?**

**参考答案**：Seeing that many plastic bottles littered the parks and sidewalks. / He grew upset seeing that many plastic bottles littered the parks and sidewalks.

**解析**：答案线索在第二段第二句"He loved biking around his neighbourhood but grew upset seeing that many plastic bottles littered the parks and sidewalks." 直接摘录原因部分即可，也可补全为完整句子。注意时态为过去时。

**41. How does the app created by Sally and her friends help?**

**参考答案**：It helps people explore hidden places like small local parks and gardens in their own cities. / The app maps small local parks and gardens few people knew about, helping people explore hidden places.

**解析**：答案线索在倒数第二段"she wondered: what if we could build a real-world app that helps people explore hidden places in their own cities?" 以及"an app that maps small local parks and gardens few people knew about"。注意将主动语态转换为app作主语的句子。

**42. Please decide which part is false in the following statement, then underline it and explain why.**

**陈述**：With encouragement from his friends and parents, Mark built a small bike shelter for his neighbourhood after months of trial and error.

**参考答案**：
- **错误部分**：*With encouragement from his friends and parents*
- **解释**：According to the passage, friends laughed a little, and even Mark's parents thought he should just give up. So Mark faced doubt from his friends and parents, not encouragement. / The passage says "Friends laughed a little, and even Mark's parents thought he should just give up", which is the opposite of "encouragement".

**解析**：第三段明确说"Friends laughed a little, and even Mark's parents thought he should just give up"，这与题干中的"encouragement"完全矛盾。划线要精准到"With encouragement from his friends and parents"，解释要直接引用原文的"laughed"和"thought he should just give up"作为反证。

**43. How can you put your imagination into practice as Mark and Sally did?**

**参考答案**：
- **One possible version**: First, identify a problem or an idea. Then, experiment bravely, learn relevant skills if needed, and keep trying despite difficulties.
- **Another version**: When I identify a problem, I try to solve it and do something practical. I would stick to my own ideas, seek help from friends, and make use of external resources. Eventually, I turn my ideas into reality.
- **Another version**: I should observe life carefully, find a real problem, and then take action to solve it. I need to be persistent and learn necessary skills, just like Mark and Sally did.

**解析**：结合Mark和Sally的故事提炼共性：发现问题/产生想法→勇敢尝试/学习技能→坚持不懈/利用资源。先构思内容（发现问题、解决问题、坚持、求助），再完成书写，注意控制在40词左右。使用First, Then等连接词使步骤清晰。

## 第六部分 备考建议

1. **精读近5年真题**：2021-2025年的5篇真题覆盖了说明文、记叙文两种主要体裁，务必逐题精做，对照满分答案分析差距。
2. **建立"阅读表达错题本"**：按"信息遗漏/语法错误/逻辑偏差/字数失控"四类归类，每周复盘。
3. **限时训练**：每天1篇，严格限时10分钟（阅读2分钟+答题8分钟），培养考场节奏感。
4. **面批面改**：开放题（43题）必须找老师面批，明确"内容分"和"语言分"的得失点。
5. **背诵积累**：背诵5-8篇满分答案，积累"Firstly, Besides, Therefore, In this way"等连接词和"It proves that... This means..."等总结句式。
